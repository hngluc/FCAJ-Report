> Here you can freely contribute your personal opinions regarding your experiences while participating in the First Cloud AI Journey program, helping the FCAJ team improve outstanding shortcomings based on the following categories:

### General Evaluation

**1. Working Environment** The learning and working environment at FCJ is highly dynamic, open-minded, and deeply driven by a tech-centric spirit. Since the program flexibly combines online schedules and offline meetups, team members are exceptionally proactive in connecting and ready to support one another regardless of time or location. The community's shared co-working space is tidy and comfortable, which strongly motivates deep focus. However, it would be great if the organizers could add a few casual gatherings or small-scale team-bonding activities between separate cohorts so that members have more opportunities to understand each other better.

**2. Mentor / Team Admin Support** The program's Mentor and Admin teams are incredibly dedicated. The intensive Lab documentation and cloud environments are allocated very systematically according to our learning pace. I particularly appreciate the Mentor's coaching mindset: instead of immediately providing answers to system bugs, the Mentor always guides and encourages us to inspect logs and perform Root Cause Analysis (RCA) to find solutions ourselves. This approach significantly enhances our independent debugging and troubleshooting skills.

**3. Alignment Between Tasks and Academic Major** The real-world project assigned during the program (Building an event-driven Smart Image Platform utilizing Serverless architecture) completely aligns with and strongly reinforces the Software Engineering major I am pursuing at university. I not only had the chance to solidify my backend programming and database design logic but also pushed my boundaries by directly configuring virtual private networks (VPC), serverless computing functions (Lambda), and integrating Artificial Intelligence modules (Amazon Rekognition).

**4. Opportunities for Learning & Skill Development** Throughout the 3-month journey, I have grown not only technically in Cloud Engineering but also immensely in soft skills. I have mastered time management during self-study phases, learned how to systematically structure source code and author clean project guides (Documentation) on GitHub, as well as how to communicate and share tech triatures by writing technical blogs.

**5. Culture & Team Spirit** The culture of the AWS First Cloud Journey program is highly positive, promoting a "Think Big" mindset and a strong sense of ownership in tasks. There is no hierarchical gap between mentors and interns; every piece of feedback regarding system architecture or source code optimization is respected and discussed openly. When facing difficult Lab scenarios or tight project deadlines, the entire team always stands unified, staying up late to troubleshoot system issues together, which creates a highly cohesive team bond.

**6. Policies / Benefits for Interns** The program sponsored a dedicated portion of cloud credits and sandboxed accounts so we could practice hands-on labs comfortably. More importantly, the privilege of attending deep-dive technology workshops hosted by AWS experts and the direct career-matching pipelines with a large network of AWS partners and clients upon graduation are invaluable benefits for a final-year student.


### Other Considerations

* **What are you most satisfied with during your internship?** What I am most satisfied with is the profound transformation in my engineering mindset. Moving from a pure programming student, I have become capable of designing and operating a complete cloud native system following an Event-Driven architecture, while learning how to optimize runtime performance and manage cloud environment budgets strictly.
* **What areas do you think the company needs to improve for future interns?** The organizing team could implement a more integrated, automated budget early-warning alert system for students' Lab accounts. This would proactively prevent instances where interns accidentally deploy infinite computing loops in their code, causing credits to burn out rapidly from the very first weeks.
* **If recommending to friends, would you advise them to intern here? Why?** Absolutely yes. This is the most practical and high-quality program for any IT student wishing to pursue Cloud or DevOps tracks. The program does not just teach dry theory; it forces interns to get their hands dirty with real products and fix live bugs, thereby gaining real-world engineering experiences that textbooks cannot provide.


### Proposals & Expectations

* **What proposals do you have to improve the overall internship experience?** I hope the organizing team can increase the frequency of group-based Code Review sessions, where Mentors can dissect the source code of prominent projects to highlight unoptimized components (Technical Debt). This would help interns grasp architectural best practices much more visually.
* **Do you wish to continue participating in this program in the future?** I am highly eager to continue accompanying the program in the future in more advanced roles, or actively contribute to the community as an AWS Community Builder to support and guide subsequent generations of interns.
* **Other feedback (free to share):** I would like to extend my heartfelt gratitude to the organizing committee, the dedicated Mentors, and all FCAJ members for building such an incredibly valuable experience, which has boosted my confidence significantly on my path to becoming a professional Software and Cloud Engineer.
