# Takeaways from the "Meetup 09 - 05 - 2026" Workshop

### Purpose of the Event

- Share effective learning methods
- Provide career orientation and an engineering mindset
- Build connections and community
- Introduce new project development methodologies

### Speaker List

- **Huynh Hoang Long** - Admin of FCAJ
- **Nguyen Tuan Thinh** - DevOps/Cloud Engineer
- **Mr. Khang** - Solutions Architect (Cloud Kinetics)
- **Mrs. Thao** - Software Development

### Key Highlights

#### "Hacking" your brain to build learning motivation

- The speaker shared how to turn studying into something enjoyable rather than a source of pressure, by leveraging the brain's dopamine mechanism (similar to how games or social media work).
- Key techniques included: **breaking goals into smaller pieces, building a continuous learning streak** (similar to Duolingo or a TikTok streak), and using the fear of "breaking the streak" to stay consistent.

#### Engineering mindset and career orientation

- Emphasized the importance of understanding the foundational knowledge rather than just chasing after AI tools.
- The concept of **integrity:** working responsibly and staying honest with yourself and your project, even when no one is watching.
- Long-term vision: don't focus only on short-term salary — prioritize places that let you learn and build up real-world experience.

#### Applying AI in software development

- Introduced the **BMAD method (BMX)**, which helps optimize the workflow of working with AI from planning all the way through implementation.
- How to keep AI under control to reduce hallucinations and improve productivity by breaking work into smaller stories and managing each stage, instead of letting AI freely handle the entire project.

### What I Learned

#### The brain-hacking technique for sustainable learning

- Instead of treating studying as a burden, turn it into an enjoyable experience like playing a game. (e.g., after finishing a task or an exercise, reward yourself with something you enjoy.)
- **Execution method:** break goals into smaller pieces, build a learning "streak" to form a habit, and use small rewards to trigger the brain's natural dopamine release.

#### Engineering mindset and career orientation

- **Focus on the foundation:** in an era where AI is changing rapidly, deeply understanding the underlying fundamentals matters more than simply knowing how to use tools.
- **Long-term thinking:** don't just look at short-term salary — prioritize environments that let you learn, make mistakes, and build up real-world experience.

#### Applying AI effectively in software development

- Instead of letting AI do everything, you need to break the work into smaller stories and manage each stage to avoid AI "hallucinations" and low-quality generated code.

#### Why haven't you started working yet?

- You need to understand what AI actually is. In the age of AI, will it replace you — especially as AI is advancing so quickly, day by day, hour by hour?
- Focus on the fundamentals.
- Does AI really help you? Does it actually deliver what you need?
- If you're weak, AI will make you weaker; if you're strong, AI will make you stronger every day.

### Experience at the Event

Attending the **"AI-Ready Freshers"** workshop was a very rewarding experience. I learned a great deal from the methods shared by Mr. Long, and Mr. Thinh's sharing about his own project — on how AI is used in websites — was also excellent. In particular, Mr. Khang shared very practical knowledge relevant to today's work environment.

#### Learning from highly experienced speakers
- Break down your goals: don't stare at an overwhelming mountain of knowledge — break it into 10-30 minute tasks, or learn one AWS service at a time.
- Build your environment: winners are the ones who know how to build a learning environment (including decorating their study desk) so that every day they actually want to sit down and work.
- Focus on the foundation: the world changes fast, but the technical foundation (infrastructure, systems thinking) doesn't. Dig deep into core knowledge instead of chasing surface-level trends.
- Critical thinking ("Why"): don't just do whatever AI tells you. Always ask "Why?" about every solution and every architecture you build.
- **Long-term vision:** don't just look at your starting salary. Choose work that gives you experience, network, and opportunities to learn.

#### Applying modern tools

- The **BMX method** helps solve the problem of AI conversations "breaking" or AI losing context. Instead of dumping the entire requirement into a single chat window, this method breaks the project down into specific roles and processes.
